The Year of Effort

250117

As a software engineer I’m familiar with a term called “tech debt”. It is the idea that the shortcuts you take in code accumulate over time leading to more complexity and maintenance challenges. Like financial debt, it must eventually be “paid back”.

Effort is the application of energy, focus, and determination toward achieving a goal or completing a task. It involves actively working to overcome challenges, solve problems, or make progress, often requiring persistence and resilience. Effort can be physical, mental, or emotional, depending on the nature of the task.

Effort debt occurs when you prioritize the short-term convenience over the long-term goals with shortcuts, compromises, or deferment. Just like financial debt, effort debt accrues “interest” and must eventually be “paid back”. If you can’t afford it, don’t take on effort debt.

Effort investment occurs when you prioritize long-term goals over short-term convenience by making thoughtful, deliberate choices. Just like financial investments, effort investment “compounds” over time, creating value and reducing future work or stress. By making intentional, strategic decisions today, you build a foundation that supports sustained progress and minimizes the need to “pay back” effort later.
